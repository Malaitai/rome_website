Achievement Standard 91880 (1.4)

Level 1 Achieve

  • Using appropriate tools, techniques and design elements for the purpose and end users
  • Applying appropriate data integrity and testing procedures in the development of the outcome
  • Describe relevant implications

Examples of relevant implications

  • social
  • cultural
  • legal
  • ethical
  • intellectual property
  • privacy
  • accessibility
  • usability
  • functionality
  • aesthetics
  • sustainability and future proofing
  • end-user considerations
  • health and safety implications

Design Elements

'Design elements' may refer to colour, shape, and other elements as required by the digital media outcome, the purpose and the end users

Data integrity and Testing Procedures

Data integrity and testing should show that the media outcome functions as intended, and the the information preseneted is relevant for the purpose and end users

Level 1 Merit

  • Using information from testing procedures to improve the quality and functionality of the outcome
  • Addressing relevant implications

For Merit, the student will need to provide evidence of how the media outcome was improved using testing procedures. For example, this could be provided using annotated screenshots

Level 1 Excellence

  • Iterative improvement throughout the design, development and testing process
  • Applying design elements effectively

For Excellence, iterative improvement is required throughout the design, development and testing process.

Iterative improvement

This needs to be shown through delibrate cycles of improvement based on testing and trialling rather than just incremental development

Iterative improvement is more than just debugging or correcting errors in a non-functioning outcome. It is expected that students will produce a functioning outcome for an Achieved grade. Iterative improvement should be aimed at making a better product. This evidence needs to be provided for moderation.
